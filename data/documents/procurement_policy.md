# Procurement Request Approval Policy

**Policy Number:** FIN-POL-008  
**Version:** 3.0  
**Last Reviewed:** March 2025  
**Applies to:** All departments

## Overview

All procurement requests must follow the approval workflow below before a purchase order (PO) is issued. Requests submitted outside this workflow will be rejected by the ERP system.

## Request Categories

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Standard | Routine operational purchases | Office supplies, software licenses under renewal |
| Capital | Equipment or assets with useful life > 1 year | Machinery, vehicles, IT hardware |
| Services | Third-party labor or consulting | Contractors, auditors, marketing agencies |
| Emergency | Urgent purchases to prevent operational disruption | Replacement parts for production line down |

## Approval Workflow

### Step 1 — Request Submission

Employee submits a Purchase Requisition (PR) in the ERP with:
- Item description and quantity
- Preferred vendor (if known)
- Business justification
- Cost center and GL code
- Required delivery date

**System validation:** PR cannot proceed without a valid cost center and budget availability check.

### Step 2 — Manager Approval

Direct manager reviews and approves or rejects within 2 business days.

- Rejection requires a written reason in the PR comments.
- Manager cannot approve their own PR (segregation of duties).

### Step 3 — Department Head Approval

Required for all requests where:
- Total value exceeds **$5,000**, OR
- Category is Capital or Services, OR
- Vendor is new (not in approved vendor list)

Department Head must approve within 3 business days.

### Step 4 — Finance Review

Finance team (procurement@company.com) reviews:
- Budget alignment
- Vendor compliance (W-9, insurance, SOC 2 for SaaS)
- Three-quote requirement for purchases over **$10,000**

Finance may return the PR for additional documentation.

### Step 5 — Executive Approval

CFO approval required when:
- Total value exceeds **$50,000**
- Multi-year contracts
- Emergency purchases over **$25,000**

CEO co-approval required for contracts exceeding **$250,000**.

### Step 6 — PO Issuance

Approved PRs are converted to POs automatically. Buyer assigns PO number and sends to vendor within 1 business day of final approval.

## Emergency Procurement

Emergency requests bypass Steps 3–4 initially but require retroactive approval within 5 business days. Document the emergency reason (equipment failure, safety incident, regulatory deadline).

## Prohibited Purchases

- Personal items on company accounts
- Gifts to government officials
- Cryptocurrency or unregulated financial instruments
- Vendors on the Restricted Vendor List (see Appendix B)

## Audit Trail

All approval actions are logged with timestamp, approver ID, and IP address. Retention period: 7 years.
